# GPT Image 2.0 Movie Poster Typography Prompts

This file focuses on title treatment, billing blocks, tagline placement, typography hierarchy, and premium poster layout direction.

## Large Title Treatments

```text
Create a theatrical movie poster with an oversized centered title in elegant condensed serif typography. Keep the title sharp, cinematic, and highly legible, positioned across the lower third with subtle tracking and premium spacing. Add a short tagline at the top center, a restrained billing block at the bottom, and keep the image composition clean enough for professional typography placement. The overall feeling should be high-end, official, and award-season ready.
```

```text

Place actor names in small caps above the image, a short teaser line above the title, and a realistic billing block below. The composition should feel like a premium studio one-sheet.
```

```text

Add a subtle one-line tagline near the center or upper third and keep the credit block extremely restrained at the bottom. The overall page should feel elegant, quiet, literary, and international arthouse in tone.
```

```text

The type should feel curated rather than commercial, with balanced margins, premium kerning, and subtle contrast against the image. No clutter, no cheap graphic effects, no overloaded text.
```

## Typography-aware Composition Prompt

```text
Create a movie poster composition specifically designed for typography. Leave clean negative space at the top for a tagline, reserve the lower third for a large title, and leave a narrow footer area for billing credits. Avoid placing complex image details behind key text zones. The final result should feel like a professionally art-directed poster mockup ready for studio typography.
```
